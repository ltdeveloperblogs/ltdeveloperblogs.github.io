Frequently Asked Questions
Understanding how search engines and automated bots view your website is crucial for maintaining a healthy, high-ranking online presence.
Browse our frequently asked questions below to learn more about technical audits and web scraping.
A technical SEO audit analyzes your website's infrastructure to ensure search engines can properly crawl, index, and understand your content. It identifies backend issues like broken links, slow page speeds, mobile responsiveness, and sitemap errors.
Bots and web scrapers use automated scripts to read your website's underlying code. Good bots, like Googlebot, rely on your robots.txt file and XML sitemaps, while malicious scrapers may ignore these rules and copy your content.
Run a quick audit monthly to catch errors early. Perform a deep audit every 6 months or after major site changes such as migrations or redesigns.
Web crawling is done by search engines to discover and index URLs. Web scraping extracts specific data such as text, images, or product prices from pages.
You can block malicious scrapers using robots.txt, server-level rate limiting, or a Web Application Firewall (WAF) like Cloudflare to detect and block suspicious traffic.
Some sites use JavaScript rendering, dynamic loading, or anti-bot protections. In such cases, you may need headless browser scraping or API-based extraction.
Legality depends on the site's terms of service and jurisdiction. Scraping publicly available data is often allowed, but copying copyrighted or private content without permission can lead to legal issues.
Competitors, aggregators, or bots may target your site for data. Monitor server logs, set up rate limits, and use CAPTCHAs or WAF rules to reduce unwanted scraping.
Scraping itself doesn’t harm SEO, but duplicate content appearing elsewhere can dilute rankings. Protect your content with canonical tags and monitoring tools.

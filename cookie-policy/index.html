1. Introduction  

This Cookie Policy explains how LT Developer Blogs uses cookies and similar tracking technologies when you visit our website. It explains what these technologies are, why we use them, and your rights to control our use of them. This policy works in conjunction with our standard Privacy Policy.

2. What Are Cookies?  

Cookies are small data files that are placed on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. They are widely used by website owners in order to make their websites work, or to work more efficiently, as well as to provide reporting information.

Cookies set by the website owner (in this case, LT Developer Blogs) are called “first-party cookies.” Cookies set by parties other than the website owner are called “third-party cookies.” Third-party cookies enable third-party features or functionality to be provided on or through the website (e.g., advertising, interactive content, and analytics).

3. How We Use Cookies  

As outlined in our Privacy Policy, we use cookies and web beacons to:

  • Store information about visitors’ preferences.
  • Record user-specific information on which pages you access or visit.
  • Customize and personalize our web page content based on your browser type or other information sent via your browser.
  • Ensure the proper functioning of our consent management platform.

4. Types of Cookies We Use  

We utilize the following categories of cookies on our blog:

Necessary / Essential Cookies  

These cookies are strictly necessary to provide you with services available through our website and to use some of its features. For example, these cookies allow our consent management system to remember your cookie preferences so you are not asked repeatedly every time you visit a new page.

Analytics and Performance Cookies  

These cookies collect information that is used in aggregate form to help us understand how our website is being used. We partner with third-party analytics providers to track metrics such as visitor count, bounce rate, and traffic sources, which helps us improve our content and site performance.

Third-Party and Advertising Cookies  

We integrate third-party services, such as comment systems, to allow user engagement on our posts. Additionally, third-party ad servers or networks may use technology (like cookies or conversion tracking scripts via a dataLayer) to deliver personalized advertisements. We have no access to or control over these cookies that are used by third-party advertisers.

5. Managing Your Consent and Preferences  

You have the right to decide whether to accept or reject non-essential cookies.

When you first visit our blog, you are presented with a cookie consent banner that allows you to customize your preferences. You can choose to accept all cookies, reject non-essential cookies, or customize your choices by category (e.g., Analytics).

If you choose to reject cookies, you may still use our website, though your access to some functionality and areas of our website (like embedded comment sections) may be restricted.

6. Updates to This Policy  

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time in order to reflect changes to the cookies we use or for other operational, legal, or regulatory reasons. Please revisit this page regularly to stay informed about our use of cookies and related technologies.

7. Contact Us  

If you have any questions about our use of cookies or other technologies, please contact us via our main domain or repository channels.
